Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think and how you act. Fortunately, it is also treatable. Depression causes feelings of sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed. It can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems and can decrease your ability to function at work and at home.

About Project

Abstract


Depression is a wide internal health concern that frequently co-occurs with colorful physical conditions, emphasizing the need for an intertwined approach to healthcare. This exploration report delves into the operation of machine and deep learning ways to predict both depression and related physical conditions. By analyzing different datasets comprising medical records, cerebral assessments, and life factors, the study aims to develop predictive models that offer early intervention and acclimatized care, eventually perfecting patient issues and healthcare resource allocation. Using the power of advanced machine learning and deep learning ways, this paper focuses on two critical aspects of patient care the early discovery of depression and the prediction of affiliated physical conditions. Through the integration of different healthcare data sources, including electronic health records, inheritable labels, and life data, the paper has developed accurate and robust predictive models for both depression and physical conditions. The presented ensemble double classifier performed comparably better than each birth algorithm in all measures and all trials. We trained the ensemble model on the reused NHANES dataset, tested, and estimated the results of its performance against internal screen force and bandied the similar prognostications.
Keywords — Major depressive disorder, Ensemble classifier, Supervised machine learning

Motivation

🔵Develop predictive models to identify individuals at risk of depression, allowing for early intervention and tailored support.
🔵Reduce the strain on healthcare systems by addressing mental health issues proactively, improving patient outcomes, and combatting mental health stigma.
🔵Generate valuable data-driven insights to inform evidence-based healthcare policies, promoting a healthier and happier society.

Machine Learning

Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables systems to automatically learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed. It involves algorithms that learn patterns and make predictions or decisions based on data. By analyzing large datasets, machine learning algorithms can uncover insights and make predictions or decisions with high accuracy. TensorFlow

TensorFlow plays a pivotal role in the project "Depression and Disease Prediction Using Machine and Deep Learning." It serves as the core technology for developing, training, and deploying machine and deep learning models with a primary focus on predicting depression and related physical diseases. Data preprocessing is another key area where TensorFlow is employed, enabling feature scaling, data augmentation, and normalization to prepare the data for model training.

Python

Python serves as the cornerstone of the project "Depression Analyzer." It is used throughout the project's lifecycle, providing a flexible and powerful programming environment for various tasks. In model development, Python is indispensable for creating machine and deep learning models that are designed to detect depression and predict related physical diseases. These models include intricate neural network architectures, and Python's extensive support for deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch ensures the implementation of complex model structures tailored to healthcare data.

Flask

Flask, a micro web framework for Python, plays a crucial role in the project "Depression Aalyzer”. It serves as the primary tool for developing web-based applications to deploy predictive models and make them accessible to healthcare professionals and end-users. One of the key applications of Flask in the project is the creation of web interfaces that allow healthcare professionals or patients to interact with the predictive models in real-time. These web applications provide user-friendly interfaces where medical practitioners can input patient data and receive predictions regarding depression risk and the likelihood of related physical diseases.
